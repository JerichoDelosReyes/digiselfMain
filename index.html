Everything begins with understanding.

We are students from Cavite State University-Imus Campus.

We are a collaborative group of BSCS2E students tasked to create a comprehensive website exploring The Digital Self. Our project focuses on understanding the complex aspects of digital identity and the systems that shape them in our modern connected world.

Through our academic research and collaborative approach, we aim to provide meaningful insights into digital identity, combining theoretical knowledge with practical understanding to create accessible educational content.

As students, we're building our understanding from the ground up, conducting thorough research and analysis to present evidence-based findings about digital identity and its impact on society.

Our project explores how digital behavior, online presence patterns, and technology reflect our authentic selves‚Äîcreating valuable insights that help individuals better understand their digital identity and navigate online spaces more consciously.

References & Research

Our research is grounded in peer-reviewed studies, expert insights, and comprehensive analysis of digital identity and mental health.

Digital Identity Research

  • Turkle, S. (2011). Alone Together: Why We Expect More from Technology and Less from Each Other. Basic Books.
  • Baym, N. K. (2015). Personal Connections in the Digital Age. Polity Press.
  • Boyd, D. (2014). It's Complicated: The Social Lives of Networked Teens. Yale University Press.

Social Media & Mental Health

  • Primack, B. A., et al. (2017). Social media use and perceived social isolation among young adults in the U.S. American Journal of Preventive Medicine, 53(1), 1-8.
  • Hunt, M. G., et al. (2018). No more FOMO: Limiting social media decreases loneliness and depression. Journal of Social and Clinical Psychology, 37(10), 751-768.
  • Weinstein, E. (2018). The social media see-saw: Positive and negative influences on adolescents. Pediatrics, 141(3), e20173584.

Digital Ethics & Privacy
